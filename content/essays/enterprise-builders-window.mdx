Benioff started Salesforce at 35 after 13 years at Oracle. Yuan built Zoom at 41. In enterprise, experience is the product.

Silicon Valley worships the young founder. But look at the data in enterprise software, and a different pattern emerges. The most enduring enterprise companies were built by people with deep industry experience, usually in their late thirties or forties.

## The Pattern

Marc Benioff spent 13 years at Oracle before starting Salesforce at 35. Eric Yuan spent 14 years at Cisco/WebEx before starting Zoom at 41. Aneel Bhusri spent over a decade at PeopleSoft before co-founding Workday at 40.

This isn't coincidence. In enterprise, the product isn't just code. It's understanding. Understanding how large organizations buy, deploy, and depend on technology. Understanding the politics, the procurement cycles, the integration nightmares, the change management.

## Why This Window Exists

By forty, you've seen enough cycles to recognize patterns. You've built enough relationships to have distribution. You've made enough mistakes to have judgment. And you still have enough energy and runway to build something that compounds for twenty years.

The window isn't about age. It's about the intersection of experience, energy, and ambition. For most enterprise builders, that intersection happens between forty and fifty.

I'm forty. The window is open.
