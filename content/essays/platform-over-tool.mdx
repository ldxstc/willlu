Every lasting enterprise company is a platform. Here's how to know if you're building one.

After watching over a hundred enterprise AI outcomes—some spectacular, most mediocre—one pattern is unmistakable. The tools get replaced. The platforms compound.

## The Difference

A tool solves a problem. A platform creates a context in which problems get solved. Salesforce isn't a CRM tool—it's the context in which customer relationships are managed. Slack isn't a messaging tool—it's the context in which work communication happens.

The distinction matters because it determines your trajectory. Tools compete on features. Platforms compete on ecosystem. Features are easy to copy. Ecosystems are not.

## The Test

Ask yourself: if a customer uses your product for two years, is it harder or easier for them to leave? If the answer is easier—or the same—you're building a tool. If it's dramatically harder, you might be building a platform.

The judgment layer that learns, the workflows that accumulate, the integrations that deepen—these are the signs of a platform emerging.
