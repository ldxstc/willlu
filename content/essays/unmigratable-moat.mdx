The most durable competitive advantage in AI isn't data or models. It's a judgment layer that learns you over time.

Every AI company claims a data moat. Most don't have one. Data is increasingly commoditized. Models are converging. The real moat is something subtler: a system that develops judgment specific to your context.

## What Judgment Means

Judgment isn't accuracy. A model can be 99% accurate and still lack judgment. Judgment is knowing which of the accurate answers is the right one for this specific customer, this specific context, this specific moment.

It's the difference between a search engine that returns relevant results and an assistant that knows what you actually need.

## Why It's Unmigratable

When a system develops judgment about your organization—your communication patterns, your decision-making style, your unstated preferences—switching costs become existential. It's not that the new system can't learn. It's that the learning took two years, and you can't afford to start over.

This is the moat. Not data. Not models. Accumulated judgment.
