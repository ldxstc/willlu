Everyone talks about AI enabling tiny teams. Nobody talks about the prerequisite: a decade of trust you can't shortcut.

The narrative is seductive: one person, armed with AI tools, can now build what used to require fifty. And it's partly true. The tooling is extraordinary. But the narrative skips the hardest part.

Enterprise buyers don't buy tools. They buy trust. And trust is the one thing you can't compress with technology.

## The Trust Prerequisite

I've watched dozens of solo founders try to sell enterprise software. The ones who succeed share one trait: they already had the relationships. They spent a decade at Google, or McKinsey, or inside the industry they're selling to. They didn't build trust with their product. They built trust with their career.

The AI tools let them execute faster. But the distribution—the ability to get a meeting, close a deal, expand an account—that came from years of compounding credibility.

## What This Means

If you're planning to build a one-person enterprise company, the best time to start was ten years ago. The second best time is now—but only if you're willing to invest the decade of trust-building that makes the model work.

The AI handles the building. You handle the believing.
