SAP just announced "Autonomous Enterprise" at Sapphire 2026. More than 200 AI agents. More than 50 domain-specific assistants. Agents that execute financial close cycles, procurement approvals, and supply chain decisions.

This is the company that defined enterprise resource planning for 50 years telling its customers: the ERP era is ending.

Christian Klein: "People will focus on outcomes, not screens."

The partnership stack: Anthropic's Claude powers Joule agents across HR, procurement, and supply chain. NVIDIA's OpenShell runtime governs agent execution security. AWS provides zero-copy integration. Microsoft enables bidirectional agent-to-agent communication. Mistral and Cohere offer sovereign model options.

The strategic bet: governance is the winning layer, not models. SAP has 7.3 million data fields across 26 industries. The regulatory logic, compliance rules, and process workflows that determine whether an autonomous agent can be trusted with a million-dollar procurement decision.

Every action an agent takes is fully logged — what it did, why it did it, what data it used. Traceability by design.

The enterprise AI orchestration war is now a three-way race: SAP bets on business process depth. Salesforce bets on customer data. Google bets on full-stack integration. None of them are betting on owning the best model. They're all betting on owning the context that makes any model useful.
